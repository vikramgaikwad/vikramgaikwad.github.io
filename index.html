About Me

Welcome to my website! I am Vikram Gaikwad, a passionate and curious researcher exploring the mysteries of chromatin organization. I believe that scientific research is not just a job, but a way of life. It drives me to push the boundaries of our knowledge and to understand the fundamental principles that govern the world around us.

My journey as a scientist started when I joined the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research Bhopal for my BS-MS dual degree program in Physics. I was intrigued by the physics of soft matter, which led me to explore the dynamics of active polymers during my MS thesis. This experience sparked my interest in computational modeling and simulation, which I further pursued by joining the Ph.D. program at the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay. My research focuses on understanding the chromatin organization inside the nucleus, which is critical for predicting gene regulation and other cellular processes. I use tools from soft-condensed matter physics and statistical mechanics to simulate chromatin polymer, predict its properties, and build models that are consistent with experimental observations.

I am thrilled to be a part of the scientific community that is constantly evolving, pushing the boundaries of our understanding, and finding solutions to complex problems that impact society. When I am not in the lab, you can find me exploring new hiking trails, capturing the beauty of nature through my lens, and unleashing my creativity through painting and sketching.

Please feel free to explore my website to learn more about my research, interests, and experiences. Don't hesitate to contact me if you have any questions or would like to collaborate.
